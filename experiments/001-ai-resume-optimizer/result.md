# Experiment 001: AI resume optimizer

> Status: **Inconclusive, stopped**
> Period: June 30, 2026 to July 6, 2026 | Revenue: CNY 0 | Evidence: `self-test`

## Hypothesis

A small Chinese-language resume optimization tool could attract job seekers through GitHub and convert some of them to a CNY 9.9 paid optimization offer.

## What was built

- A local Flask MVP.
- Resume text input and AI-assisted rewrite flow.
- ATS-style feedback and a basic paid-offer/fake-door concept.
- Local author testing of the primary product path.

## What was actually observed

- The author could run and exercise the MVP locally.
- No external acquisition campaign was executed.
- No independently measured GitHub traffic was attached.
- No qualified lead, checkout, payment, or customer interview evidence was recorded.
- Revenue recorded for the experiment is CNY 0.

## Missing evidence

| Question | Evidence available |
|---|---|
| Does the MVP execute? | Author self-test only |
| Did relevant users visit? | None |
| Did users request or purchase the service? | None |
| Was any revenue verified? | No |
| What did acquisition cost? | Unknown |

The original idea included an optimization loop and fake-door test, but those plans are not counted as observed behavior.

## Conclusion

The product implementation hypothesis received limited local support: a runnable MVP existed. The acquisition and willingness-to-pay hypotheses were not exercised. It would be inaccurate to call the business validated, and it would also be inaccurate to call the market test a verified failure.

The correct classification is **inconclusive**.

A future rerun would need:

1. a defined audience and channel;
2. tagged landing-page traffic;
3. a predeclared conversion metric;
4. a bounded time or spend stop rule;
5. privacy-safe evidence for visits, leads, and payments.

The authoritative machine-readable record is [experiment.json](experiment.json). The historical MVP remains in this folder for reproducibility, not as proof of demand.
